# ADR-0070: Holo Trade — a self-sovereign trading terminal over Hyperliquid: read the order book with no server, sign every order with the user's own key through the default-deny wallet gate, delegate fast trading to a withdrawal-less agent wallet that is a revocable UCAN, and seal each fill as a post-quantum, content-addressed receipt that re-derives to the exact computation it settles. No new exchange code is written — the official Hyperliquid SDK does all signing and encoding; Hologram supplies only the identity, the conscience gate, and the proof.

**Status:** Proposed — to be witnessed by `tools/holo-trade-witness.mjs` (pure Node, offline; the live `POST /exchange` and the WebSocket are *mocked* exactly as ADR-0048's rail and ADR-0068's broadcast are mocked) and a required `#holo-trade` row in `os/etc/conformance.jsonld` (the gate, ADR-0024), with a live-Chromium `#holo-trade-render` row (the order book renders; a signed order *intent* forms without broadcast) and a `holo-trade` row in `specs.json` (ADR-0031). The runtime is a new holospace app `Hologram Apps/apps/trade` plus a thin shared data lib promoted **verbatim** from what already exists — the read-only HyperCore Info/WebSocket layer (`holo-hypercore.js`, `holo-hypercore-stream.js`) the Holo Scan / etherscan app already ships as a markets terminal — and the **vendored, sealed** Hyperliquid TS SDK for the write side. Routes every signature through the **already-built** Holo Wallet seam (`os/usr/lib/holo/holo-wallet-bridge.js`) — default-deny, human-approval, no new crypto. The decision is not done until it is witnessed (ADR-0024); this ADR pins the design and carries the build spec. *(House-keeping: `docs/adr/` numbering currently has collisions — `0067` (`holo-qvac`/`holo-route`) and `0069` (`holo-bittensor`/`holo-route` in the index) are each duplicated; `0070` was chosen as unambiguously free on disk and in the index. Re-grep the max before committing, since numbers advance concurrently.)* Composes Holo Login's seed identity (the BIP-39 → EVM key keystone), Holo Wallet's signing seam (ADR-0068), Holo Delegate (ADR-0042, the UCAN authorization layer), Holo Settle (ADR-0048) + its Plasma rail (ADR-0068), Holo Orchestrate (ADR-0045, the PROV-O work receipt), the Holo Constitution (ADR-0033, the pre-dispatch gate), Holo Own's anchor-by-reference (ADR-0053), and the UOR envelope + `verifyDeep` (ADR-0025). **Mints nothing** beyond a new `hostrade:` namespace, one sealed chain descriptor, and the sealed vendored SDK object.

**Context.** A holospace user already *is* an on-chain account: the Holo Login keystone derives one BIP-39 seed into a self-sovereign EVM key, and Hyperliquid — a high-performance, fully on-chain order-book DEX for perpetuals and spot — authenticates by **signature, not by API secret**. Its `info` endpoint (market data, account state) and its WebSocket (`allMids`, `l2Book`, `trades`, `userFills`, `orderUpdates`) are CORS-enabled and key-less; its `exchange` endpoint (place/cancel orders, transfers, approvals) takes a *signed action* and nothing else. This is the rare venue whose trust model is already the substrate's: **self-custody, verify-don't-trust, the key never leaves the device.** The read half is, in fact, *already built and shipping*: `apps/etherscan/_shared/holo-hypercore.js` reads `metaAndAssetCtxs`, `l2Book`, `recentTrades`, `clearinghouseState`, `userFills`; `holo-hypercore-stream.js` holds one auto-reconnecting socket multiplexing the live subscriptions; `holo-omni.js` already folds a HyperCore `accountValue` into the verifiable omnichain portfolio. What is missing is the **intent surface** — the ability to *act*, not just watch — and the discipline to make acting safe, sovereign, and provable.

Three perils make this an ADR and not a patch. First, **signing is unforgiving and must never be hand-rolled.** Hyperliquid's L1 actions are msgpack-encoded (field-order- and trailing-zero-sensitive, addresses lowercased), hashed to a `connectionId`, and signed under an EIP-712 "phantom agent" domain; user-signed actions (`approveAgent`, `withdraw3`, `spotSend`, `approveBuilderFee`) use explicit typed-data structures. The official documentation is blunt: *"Never hand-roll signatures — use SDK methods."* Writing our own encoder would be both a bug farm and a violation of NIHITO. Second, **a trading app must never hold withdrawal power.** A long-lived key embedded in a tab to sign rapid orders is a key that can also drain the account. Third, **a chain config is a phishing surface** — swap the `exchange` URL or the bridge contract and every order, every deposit, is redirected. The design must (a) delegate *all* encoding and signing to the official SDK, sealed by content address; (b) keep the master key in the wallet behind the default-deny human gate, and grant routine trading only to a **withdrawal-less, revocable** agent key; (c) make the venue's parameters a sealed, tamper-evident object; and (d) turn every fill into a content-addressed receipt so a trade is *checkable*, not merely *reported*.

**Decision.** Build a sovereign trading terminal that re-implements nothing of Hyperliquid and adds exactly five things:

1. **The write side is the official Hyperliquid SDK, vendored and *sealed* — Hologram writes zero signing or encoding code (NIHITO, Law L4).** The canonical TypeScript SDK (`@nktkas/hyperliquid`, the de-facto browser-capable client that implements the official msgpack + phantom-agent EIP-712 scheme and accepts an **external signer**; CCXT is the documented cross-venue alternative) is vendored into `os/usr/lib/holo/hyperliquid-sdk/` and pinned by a self-verifying `…/hyperliquid-sdk.uor.json` (ADR-0025): its κ commits to every shipped byte (RFC 8785 / SRI), so a tampered SDK changes the κ and is **refused at load** (Law L5). All order construction, cancellation, and the entire signing pipeline — msgpack canonicalization, the `connectionId` hash, the EIP-712 domain — are the SDK's, verbatim. The read side is the **already-shipping** HyperCore Info/WS layer, promoted from `apps/etherscan/_shared/` to a shared lib unchanged. We strictly stick to the SDK and the chain spec; we add only the wiring around them.

2. **Hyperliquid is a *sealed* chain descriptor, so the venue itself is content-addressed and un-swappable (anti-phishing is structural, Law L1).** `os/etc/holo-chains/hyperliquid.uor.json` is a self-verifying UOR object pinning, verbatim from Hyperliquid's official endpoints page: the `info` and `exchange` base `https://api.hyperliquid.xyz`, the WebSocket `wss://api.hyperliquid.xyz/ws`, the testnet mirrors, **HyperEVM** as `caip2: "eip155:999"` (`chainId 999` / `0x3e7`, native `HYPE`, RPC `https://rpc.hyperliquid.xyz/evm`), and the **Arbitrum Bridge2** USDC contract address used for deposits/withdrawals — each pinned *verbatim from the official page and re-verified by the human before sealing*. The descriptor's κ commits to all of it; a swapped RPC, exchange URL, or bridge address changes the κ and is refused. HyperEVM, being EVM, is registered into the existing WDK chain registry as one entry, exactly as ADR-0068 registered Plasma — **no new chain implementation, no new key derivation** (the seed identity already speaks EVM).

3. **Every signature crosses the default-deny human gate; routine trading runs on a withdrawal-less agent wallet that *is* a revocable UCAN (Law L4 + ADR-0042).** Two signing paths, both through `holo-wallet-bridge.js`, neither ever exposing the master key to the app:
   - **The one-time grant (human-gated, user-signed action).** The user approves a single `approveAgent` — a Hyperliquid *agent wallet* / API wallet: a separate key that may sign **orders and cancels but can never withdraw or transfer**. The SDK builds the typed-data; the wallet signs it behind the one consent dialog; default-deny if the wallet is closed or the user declines. Hologram wraps that grant as a **Holo Delegate UCAN** (ADR-0042): a content-addressed, *attenuated* (trade-only, no `withdraw3`/`spotSend`), *time-boxed*, *revocable* capability whose principal alignment and attenuation re-derive. Revoking the UCAN (and re-keying the agent on-chain) ends fast trading instantly — the substrate's revocation and Hyperliquid's agent model express the same intent.
   - **The fast path (delegated, no per-order dialog).** Orders are signed by the agent key the grant authorized — checked against the UCAN's attenuation on every action (`authorizeStep`, ADR-0042) — so a holospace literally *cannot* sign a withdrawal even if compromised, and cannot trade past the grant's expiry or revocation. Withdrawals and transfers always fall back to path one: the master key, the human gate. The key that can lose money never lives in the tab; the key in the tab can only trade, briefly, by permission you can withdraw.

4. **Every order passes the conscience gate before dispatch, and every fill becomes a sealed PROV-O receipt (Law L5 + ADR-0033 + ADR-0045).** Before the SDK signs, the action passes the Constitution's pre-dispatch gate (ADR-0033): operator-set, content-addressed risk limits (max notional, max leverage, per-market caps, a daily loss stop) are conscience obligations — an order that violates one is **blocked, unsigned**, with a re-derivable verdict, not a soft warning. After a fill returns, Holo Trade seals a `hostrade:Trade` object: a PROV-O entity (`prov:wasGeneratedBy` the signed action; `prov:used` the market snapshot κ) carrying the order, the fill (price, size, fee, the venue tx hash as `holo://0x…`), the builder code, and the conscience verdict — its κ a content commitment to the whole. A multi-leg strategy is a Holo Orchestrate execution DAG (ADR-0045) whose root κ proves the sequence. **Your position history stops being a broker's statement and becomes self-verifying provenance**: anyone with the κ re-derives the order, the authorizing UCAN, the conscience verdict, and the on-chain fill — the open-web audit trail Holo AgentTrust and Holo Orchestrate were built to make checkable, now reaching the trade.

5. **The entitlement and the audit trail are post-quantum, even though Hyperliquid's signature is not — and we are honest about exactly where the line falls.** This is where the UOR substrate makes the system *more* secure than the venue. The parts an adversary must not be able to forge — the **receipt** (this fill, at this price, under this authority), the **delegation chain**, and the **provenance** — are hash-based content commitments (sha-256, RFC 8785), which are quantum-resistant (Grover gives only a quadratic speedup ⇒ ~128-bit residual security), and the `holo-vc` signature seam is swappable to a post-quantum scheme (ML-DSA / SPHINCS+) to make the *Hologram* attestation over each order PQ without touching this design. What we do **not** claim: Hyperliquid mandates classical secp256k1 ECDSA for the action signature and classical consensus for finality, and we cannot and do not change its chain spec — its on-chain authorization is classical, exactly as ADR-0068 conceded Plasma's finality is classical. The substrate guarantees the *entitlement and the audit*; the venue guarantees the *matching and the custody of margin*. Stating that split honestly is the point — and it lets a future PQ co-signature upgrade the half we control.

**Magical — for the trader and for the guest who watches an agent trade.** The terminal renders the live book the instant it opens (the read layer is already proven), and acting is one verb over the identity you already have — no exchange account, no API keys, no server:

```js
import { HoloTrade } from "@hologram/trade";          // this ADR — wraps the sealed Hyperliquid SDK
import { hyperliquid } from "@hologram/chains";        // the sealed venue descriptor κ
import { wallet } from "@hologram/wallet";             // the default-deny signing seam (ADR-0068)

const hl = HoloTrade.connect(hyperliquid, { signer: wallet });   // key stays in the wallet

// once, human-approved: a withdrawal-less agent wallet, wrapped as a revocable UCAN (ADR-0042)
const grant = await hl.approveAgent({ ttl: "8h", scope: "trade-only", maxNotional: 25_000 });

// thereafter, fast: the conscience gate checks limits, the agent key signs, the SDK posts
const fill = await hl.order({ coin: "ETH", isBuy: true, sz: 0.5, type: "market",
                              builder: hyperliquid.builder });    // honest, transparent fee share
// fill.receiptκ re-derives → order ⊕ UCAN ⊕ conscience verdict ⊕ on-chain tx — pin it, share it, verify it

await hl.revoke(grant);    // fast trading ends instantly; the master key never moved
```

An **agent** gets the same power over MCP — `trade_markets`, `place_order`, `cancel_order`, `account_state` — but `place_order` returns a *signed intent the wallet must gate* (or runs only inside an unexpired, attenuated UCAN), never an unbounded capability: verify, don't trust. And the human experience completes the loop ADR-0064 and ADR-0068 were chasing — a guest arrives running, an agent does a slice of verifiable trading, **the instant the fill re-derives the guest watches it settle**, and (composing ADR-0068) a profit share pays out in gasless USD₮ against work that re-derives. Proof → trade → dollars, with nothing in between to trust.

**Relationship to what exists (the boundary that is actually new).** The read plane is *already shipping* and is reused verbatim — Holo Trade is the *intent surface* over the same content (Law L3: a view and an intent surface over a holospace), the way Holo Find is the intent surface over Holo Resolve. The signing and key custody are pre-existing: the BIP-39 seed (Holo Login), the WDK EVM engine and the `holo-wallet-bridge.js` default-deny seam (ADR-0068, unmodified). The authorization is pre-existing: a Holo Delegate UCAN *is* the natural wrapper for a Hyperliquid agent wallet — same attenuation, same revocation. The settlement and receipt machinery are pre-existing: a `hostrade:Trade` is a PROV-O entity the Orchestrate/Settle stack already knows how to verify and pay against. The genuinely new objects are three and their novelty is the point: the **sealed vendored SDK** (so the encoder we depend on is content-addressed, not a moving npm target), the **sealed venue descriptor** (so the endpoints are a tamper-evident fact, not an editable string), and the **`hostrade:` vocabulary** binding orders/fills/positions to schema.org + PROV-O. It mirrors **Holo Own (ADR-0053)** and **Settle-Plasma (ADR-0068)** exactly: anchor an external chain *by reference*, only when an action needs it, never minting a pillar or custody into the core (Law L4).

**Consequences.** A holospace user can trade perps and spot on Hyperliquid as their sovereign self: the book streams with no server, orders are signed by their own key through a default-deny gate, fast trading runs on a revocable withdrawal-less delegation, every order is conscience-checked before it is signed, and every fill is a content-addressed receipt that re-derives to the computation, the authority, and the on-chain tx. Three properties no raw exchange frontend offers hold: **the entitlement/audit is post-quantum** (hash-based, independent of the venue's classical signatures), **the trade is auditable to the computation** (the receipt κ binds order ⊕ UCAN ⊕ verdict ⊕ fill), and **the venue is un-phishable** (endpoints and bridge are sealed; a swap changes the κ and is refused). Honest scope and costs: (a) **Hyperliquid custodies margin and runs the matching engine** — "Hologram-native exchange" means native identity, native conscience, and native receipts *over* Hyperliquid's liquidity, not a matching engine we run; we do not claim L5 over the venue's order book or its USDC bridge (the bridge is a classical custody surface, made tamper-*evident* by the sealed descriptor, not trustless). (b) The witness proves the *signing and binding* offline — the sealed SDK signs a canonical order deterministically; a tampered order ⇒ **no** signed action; an agent UCAN signs a trade but is **refused** signing a `withdraw3` (attenuation holds); a limit-violating order is conscience-**blocked** unsigned; the fill receipt re-derives and its κ binds order ⊕ verdict ⊕ tx; no wallet open ⇒ default-deny — while the live `POST /exchange` and the socket stay mocked in the gate, as ADR-0048/0068's rail stays mocked. (c) Builder codes (`approveBuilderFee` once + `builder:{b,f}` per order) are the **honest, transparent** monetization — a disclosed fee share on routed volume, no custody, capped by the user-approved max; it is operator-opt-in, never a gate requirement. (d) A perps-DEX frontend carries jurisdictional exposure — a **product/operator decision**, surfaced, never a core dependency. It composes the catalog rather than extending it: Login's identity, Wallet's seam, Delegate's authorization, the Constitution's gate, Orchestrate's receipt, Settle's payout, Own's anchor-by-reference — minting nothing new but one vocabulary, one sealed descriptor, and the sealed SDK.
